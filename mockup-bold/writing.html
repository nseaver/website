All Algorithms Anthropology Attention

Work and the Data Economy

Together with Marcel LaFlamme and Alex Blanchette, I co-edited a special issue of Economic Anthropology collecting articles from the 2024 joint meeting of the Society for the Anthropology of Work and the Society for Economic Anthropology, and co-authored an introductory essay tracing the relationship between abstraction and contempt.

June 2025

The Mathematics of Models and the Mathematics of Measure in Southern California

The journal Social Analysis published a new translation of an essay by Claude Lévi-Strauss, and invited several commentaries. This piece, co-authored with Bill Maurer, responds to Lévi-Strauss’s call for non-quantifying mathematical work in the social sciences.

December 2024

Attention Is All You Need

This chapter in Scenes of Attention, edited by D. Graham Burnett and Justin E.H. Smith, compares two contemporary uses of attention: in popular critiques of the software industry and in AI architectures like GPT.

November 2023

Computing Taste

The people who make music recommender systems have lofty goals: they want to broaden listeners’ horizons and help obscure musicians find audiences. Drawing on years of ethnographic fieldwork, this book describes how the makers of music recommendation navigate these tensions.

December 2022

Shifting Attention

This special issue of Science, Technology & Human Values, co-edited with Rebecca Jablonsky and Tero Karppi, gathers articles that explore shifts among attention’s many meanings.

March 2022

Care and Scale

For the people who make recommender systems, the fact that care and scale seem intrinsically opposed is a problem. This article in Cultural Anthropology describes how they try to solve it.

August 2021

Everything Lies in a Space

This essay in the Journal of the Royal Anthropological Institute examines the use of spatializing techniques for analyzing cultural data in music recommendation and post-war cognitive anthropology.

April 2021

Captivating Algorithms

This article in the Journal of Material Culture explores a tendency among the makers of recommender systems to describe their purpose as “hooking” people.

December 2019

What Should an Anthropology of Algorithms Do?

This short essay in Cultural Anthropology argues against embracing the “analog slot.”

August 2018

Algorithms as Culture

This article in Big Data & Society argues that we should approach algorithms as “multiples”—unstable objects enacted through varied practices.
